University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa

It is who we are, it is what we stand for, and a reason why people should care. It is what we do, what we say, how we look—it's our shared identity. It creates pride in place.

Brand Style Guide → design_uh.json

Brand Consistency

Every element should reinforce UH Mānoa's shared identity. Use approved logos, typography, and colors.

Accessibility

Meet ADA accessibility in contrast, type size, and layout. Never use non-compliant contrast or red body type.

Quality & Legibility

Typography and layout should reflect institutional quality. Use generous leading and readable body sizes.

Pride of Place

Design supports the university's mission and creates pride in place across print, web, and digital.

5 Accessibility

Use ADA-compliant contrast ratios for all text and UI elements. Per UH brand guidelines:

  • Do not use non–ADA compliant contrast colors
  • Do not use red type for body or primary content
  • Do not make layouts too dark for readability
  • Do not create custom palettes or gradients outside the official brand

Primary green #024731 on white meets contrast requirements for large text and UI elements; use black for body text on white.
