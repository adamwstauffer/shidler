# BUS 629 — how revisions and re-grading work this semester

**Subject:** BUS 629 — how revisions and re-grading work this semester

---

Aloha BUS-629,

A few of you have already started incorporating feedback from your Stage 0 and Stage 2 grades — that's exactly the iteration habit this project is designed to build, and I want to formalize how those revisions get reflected in your grade so the rules are clear up front.

## How it works

After each stage's due date, I'll run **one revision sweep** across every student's repo. The sweep does this:

1. I check the state of your repo as of the stage's deadline.
2. I re-apply the same rubric I used the first time.
3. If your score improved (because you addressed feedback, expanded sections, fixed data, etc.), I update your grade to the new score.

**You don't need to email me or open an issue.** Just keep revising the files in your repo before the deadline. The sweep picks up whatever is there.

The full rubric applies — there's **no cap** on how much your score can move. If you revise to a perfect 100 against the rubric, your final is 100.

- **One sweep per stage.** Once the sweep runs for a given stage, that stage's score is locked. Commits after the sweep won't trigger another re-grade.
- **Sweep timing this semester:**

  | Stage | Due | Sweep |
  |---|---|---|
  | Stage 0 — Repo setup | May 16, 11:59 PM | May 17 |
  | Stages 1–3 — Template / Memo / Build | May 23, 11:59 PM | May 24 |
  | Stages 4–5 — Spec / LLM Analysis | May 30, 11:59 PM | May 31 |

## Why I'm running it this way

Originally I considered letting students request a re-grade any time, as many times as they wanted, before the stage closed. After thinking it through: **22 students × 5 stages × N re-grades per student gets out of control quickly for me as the instructor.** Batching to one sweep per stage caps the semester's review workload at a manageable number and concentrates the work at five well-defined cutoffs.

The trade-off is that you can't ping me mid-week asking *"can you check now?"* — but the upside is that the rules are the same for everyone, and you can revise as many times as you want before the deadline without using up an "attempt." Just keep iterating, and whatever is in the repo at 11:59 PM on the due date is what I'll grade.

## What this means for you, practically

- Read the feedback I left on your Stage N (in the rubric comments I sent you).
- Decide which items are worth fixing before the deliverable deadline.
- Commit those fixes to your repo. Use clear commit messages so I can see your iteration history.
- On the date of the sweep, I'll run it and update your Lamaku grades.

One small ask: when you make a revision in response to feedback, write a clear commit message describing what changed (`Expand BIO to 175 words; add long-term governance goal` is much more useful than `update bio`). This is the same audit-trail discipline you'd want on any real-world deliverable.

If you have questions about how a specific rubric item applies to your work, those are still welcome by email any time. I'm just not running per-student re-grades on demand.
